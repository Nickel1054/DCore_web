D.Core Spread Calculator

Our project was built to provide efficient up-to-date information on spreads of different commodities for D.Trading employees and clients. It provides visual data, data in table form on web page, and an option to download data on selected spread as an Excel file.


Registration

This site can be viewed by an unauthorized user, however you cannot use spreads calculator unless you are logged in. To get an account, one should contact our team with the request for a new account. When your request is processed, a new account will be created, and you will receive a confirmation email.

Note: You can only get an account if you have d.trading or dtek.com domain email.
E.g. UsernameNN@dtek.com, usernamenn@d.trading .

Contact to Register

Logging In

In a confirmation email, stating you have an account created, you will find log-in credentials. You should use them to log in on our Log in page.

WARNING! It is highly recommended to change your password immediately upon your first log in.


Calculator Form

After logging in you can proceed to use the calculator. You may click "Calculator" link on the navigation bar on the top of the page or just follow this link. This way you will land on a page with a form, which you need to fill out to receive the data about the spread you need.

The starting page will look like this:



As you can see, the first required item you need to fill out is the Commodity. After each filled item in form, next corresponding item will appear. For example, if you choose Gas as a Commodity, two fields will appear in Buy and Sell columns, named Gas Source, with gas source variants of answer.
And if you choose Electricity futures, similarly, two fields will appear in Buy and Sell columns, named Gas Source, with gas source variants of answer.


Every form field allows you to search the item you need, as shown on the image:



Note that there is a required number of fields that you need to fill out. Otherwise, the form will not be full and the request cannot be made. To prevent errors, the Submit button is unavailable until the form is fully filled. Make sure you have all the necessary fields filled to proceed. If you have troubles with the form, you can always click Reset From button.

Results

The results are shown for every available delivery year (usually from 2019 to current). To determine which product the datapoint corresponds to we provide the Delivery Year field in data. This way, if a user requested spread, one of the products of which is June 2023, Calculator will provide data for products of June 2019, June 2020, June 2021, June 2022, June 2023. It allows user to compare spreads of different years.

Results page is divided into three parts, as shown on the image below:



1. Result plots

Our project allows us to see the requested data in a form of graphs. They are dynamic, you can expand or reduce the x-coordinate (Trading Date) and on hover over the data you can see the values on any specific date. Read more on next part of documentation "The plots".

2. Table of results

You can also view the data in a form of a table. It is not very convenient, but allows user to have a brief look on the data if one needs to. We recommend to use the next part of results to get the data in a form of table.

3. Download Excel file

We provide the opportunity for our users to get the spread data in a convenient and familiar form - Excel table. Clicking Download Excel will trigger your browser to download the requested data in .xlsx file in desired or pre-defined directory on your computer (depends on a web browser you are using).

The Plots

The Plots part of results page is divided dy rows, which correspond with delivery year of a product. Each row is also divided by two columns.

The first plot in a row (the first column) shows a plot of timeseries, where on x-Axis there is Trading Date and on y-Axis there is Price (Settlement).

The second plot in a row shows a plot of spreads, where on x-Axis there is Trading Date and on y-Axis there is Spread. Here we define spread as a price difference between two products:
Spread = BUY - SELL.

You can see the example of division on the image:



On this example the first row is the Delivery Year 2021, second - Delivery Year 2022, and the third - Delivery Year 2023.
